\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
We presented a direction-level study of compositionality in residual-stream steering vectors. Across 20 instruction directions and 85 pairs, we found a mixed regime: representation-space alignment is moderate on average, reconstruction quality is often poor, and behavior-level additive gains are negligible in our evaluated subset.

The key takeaway is simple: residual directions are partially compositional but not uniformly compositional. Assuming a single global linear subspace is too strong for reliable multi-constraint steering.

Future work should (i) tune coefficients and layers per pair, (ii) repeat analyses on larger models, (iii) compare static vectors with context-dependent vector fields, and (iv) evaluate with stricter task-level scorers for long-form instruction following.
